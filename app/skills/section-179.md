# Section 179 — Accelerated Depreciation

## Plain-English summary
Section 179 lets a business **immediately deduct** the full cost of qualifying equipment and software in the year it's placed in service, instead of spreading the deduction over many years.

## 2024 limits
- **Maximum deduction**: $1,220,000
- **Phase-out threshold**: begins at $3,050,000 in total qualifying purchases
- **Taxable income limit**: deduction can't exceed the business's taxable income for the year

## Qualifying property
- Tangible personal property used in business ≥ 50% of the time
- Off-the-shelf computer software
- Certain building improvements (roofs, HVAC, fire protection, security)
- Business vehicles (with separate caps for passenger cars)

## Does NOT qualify
- Real estate (land, buildings themselves)
- Property used for lodging
- Property held for investment
- Property received as a gift or inheritance

## How to talk about it

When surfacing a §179 opportunity:
1. Lead with the dollar figure: "You have **$X,XXX** in equipment on your balance sheet that may qualify for immediate deduction."
2. Show the tax-savings range at different business-use percentages (100% / 75% / 50%).
3. Note the catch: "This is only a benefit in a profitable year — §179 can't create a loss."
4. Close with: "Confirm the business-use percentage and timing with your tax advisor before filing."

## Common mistakes to flag
- Claiming §179 on a vehicle used <50% for business
- Claiming §179 when the business had a net loss (use bonus depreciation instead)
- Confusing §179 with bonus depreciation (they can stack, but rules differ)
